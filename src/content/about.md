## About this site

This site exists mainly as a place to write things down.

I run a fairly involved home setup and, over time, I realised that the hardest part isn’t building things — it’s remembering *why* certain decisions were made months or years later. This site is where I document the systems I run, the trade-offs behind them, and the changes I make as things evolve.

Most of the writing is for my own reference. It’s public in case it’s useful to someone else.

---

## About me

I run a home server stack for my family that covers storage, backups, media, networking, and a small collection of self-hosted services. The setup has grown gradually over time, driven by real needs rather than experimentation for its own sake.

I’m less interested in chasing the newest tools and more interested in systems that stay understandable, recoverable, and boring once they’re in place. Reliability matters more to me than novelty, and I try to avoid setups that require constant attention just to keep working.

A lot of what I do revolves around making things resilient: sensible backup strategies, clear separation of services, and setups that can be rebuilt when something inevitably fails.

---

## What you’ll find here

Most of the writing on this site falls into a few categories:

- Architecture notes and overviews of how parts of my setup fit together  
- Write-ups of decisions, migrations, and changes over time  
- Setup guides for things I actually run and maintain  
- Notes and post-mortems when something breaks or doesn’t work as expected  

I try to write things down clearly enough that future-me can follow them without having to reverse-engineer my own thinking.

---

## What you won’t

This isn’t a tutorial site or a collection of quick tips copied from documentation. I don’t aim to cover everything, only the systems I personally use and understand.

You won’t find hype, benchmarks for the sake of it, or constant tool-chasing. If something appears here, it’s because it’s part of a setup I’m responsible for keeping running.
